Teriyaki Chicken Recipe

Description

The popular Japanese-inspired dish teriyaki chicken has juicy, delicate chicken covered in a savory-sweet glaze made with sake, sugar, mirin, and soy sauce. The rich, glossy sauce that gives the chicken its distinctive flavor can be brushed over after it has been perfectly pan-seared, roasted, or grilled. This dish is a tasty and filling supper that is frequently served with steamed rice and veggies.

Ingredients

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts or thighs
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce (low-sodium preferred)
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon mirin (or white wine vinegar as a substitute)
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated fresh ginger
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch (optional, for thickening)
  • 1 tablespoon water (if using cornstarch)

Steps

  1. Prepare the sauce - The soy sauce, brown sugar, honey, rice vinegar, sesame oil, mirin, ginger, and garlic should all be combined in a small saucepan. Mix thoroughly to incorporate.

  2. Simmer the sauce - Bring the mixture to a simmer in the saucepan over medium heat. Cook, stirring periodically, until the sauce thickens slightly and the sugar melts, 3 to 5 minutes.

  3. Cook the chicken - Heat a big skillet or grill pan over medium heat while the sauce is boiling. Use salt and pepper to season the chicken. The chicken should be cooked through and golden brown after 5 to 6 minutes on each side of the pan (internal temperature should reach 165°F/74°C).

  4. Coat the chicken - Lower the heat to low after the chicken is done. After adding the teriyaki sauce, sauté the chicken for a further one to two minutes so that the sauce may cover it evenly and caramelize a little.

  5. Serve - Serve the Teriyaki chicken over rice and garnish with sesame seeds and chopped green onions, if using. Enjoy!
